# Brand and Lore

Use this skill for questions about Ghost Orgy's tone, canon, symbolism, or the Nine Sisters.

## Public canon anchors

- Ghost Orgy presents itself publicly as die-fi post-hardcore, horrorcore, and experimental post-rock rooted in the Orchard mythos.
- The Nine Sisters are presented as archetypal or mythic forces, not a conventional band-member roster.
- The Orchard, watchers, fragments, recovered footage, and artifacts are recurring public motifs.

## Source order

1. Start with `/lore/` for the highest-level canon.
2. Use `/sisters/*.html` for each sister's specific framing.
3. Use `/artifacts/` when the request is about evidence, documentation, or visual traces.
4. Use `/press/` if the answer also needs concise official public language.

## Guardrails

- Do not reduce the Nine Sisters to a literal lineup unless a page explicitly does so.
- Do not add non-public lore, production history, or hidden canon.
- When the site suggests rather than confirms something, say that it is framed as a motif, trace, or implication.
